Cook County is home to over 5 million people and one of the most complicated property tax systems in the United States. If you own property in Cook County and you've never appealed your assessment, there's a good chance you're overpaying.

## How Cook County Assessments Work

The Cook County Assessor's Office reassesses properties on a triennial (3-year) cycle. The county is divided into three groups of townships:

- **City of Chicago townships** reassess every 3 years
- **North suburbs** reassess in a different year
- **South suburbs** reassess in a third year

This means your township only gets reassessed every 3 years — but you can appeal every year if you believe your current assessment is incorrect.

## The Two Levels of Appeal

### 1. Cook County Assessor's Office
After your reassessment notice arrives, you typically have **30 days** to file an appeal directly with the Assessor. This is the first and most accessible level. The process is online and relatively quick.

### 2. Board of Review
If you're not satisfied with the Assessor's decision (or if you missed the Assessor deadline), you can appeal to the **Cook County Board of Review**. Each township has its own filing window, usually in the fall.

### 3. Property Tax Appeal Board (PTAB)
If you still disagree after the Board of Review, you can escalate to the **state-level PTAB**. This takes longer (often 1–2 years) but can be worth it for larger reductions.

## What Evidence Wins Appeals

The strongest evidence in Cook County appeals is **comparable sales data** — recent arm's-length sales of properties similar to yours. Specifically:

- Sales within the last 12–18 months
- Same township
- Similar square footage (within 20%)
- Similar age and construction type
- Similar lot size

You need at least 3 good comps, ideally 5 or more. The more evidence, the better.

## What You Can Win

Successful Cook County appeals routinely reduce assessed values by 10–30%. On a home with a $10,000 annual tax bill, that's $1,000–$3,000 in savings per year.

Reductions are applied to the current tax year if you win at the Assessor or Board of Review level. PTAB decisions may result in refunds for prior years.

## The Filing Deadline Is Everything

Missing the filing window means waiting for next year's cycle. Cook County is strict about deadlines — there are very few exceptions.
