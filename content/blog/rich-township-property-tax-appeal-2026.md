Rich Township is part of Cook County's South District, currently in the 2026 reassessment cycle. If you own property in Rich Township, your assessed value is being recalculated right now — and your appeal window is open.

## Communities in Rich Township

Rich Township covers several south suburban communities including:
- **Matteson**
- **Park Forest**
- **Richton Park**
- **Olympia Fields**
- **Steger** (partial)

## Why 2026 Matters for Rich Township Homeowners

Cook County reassesses properties on a three-year rotating schedule. When your township enters a reassessment year, the Assessor recalculates your property's value from scratch. This creates both risk and opportunity.

The risk: your assessed value could jump significantly, especially in areas that have seen market appreciation.

The opportunity: you can appeal the new assessment with fresh comparable sales data and potentially lock in a lower tax bill for the next three years.

## How Much Can Rich Township Homeowners Save?

Cook County homeowners who successfully appeal their assessments typically save $800–$2,500 per year depending on property value. Over a three-year cycle, that's $2,400–$7,500 in total savings.

The key is acting during the appeal window. Once it closes, you can't appeal your current assessment at the Assessor's level.

## How to Appeal Your Rich Township Assessment

**Step 1: Check your assessment notice**
The Cook County Assessor mails notices when your township's appeal window opens. You typically have 30 days from the date on your notice.

**Step 2: Find comparable properties**
A successful appeal shows your assessed value is higher than comparable nearby properties. You need recent sales data of similar homes in your area.

**Step 3: File your appeal**
File online at the Cook County Assessor's website, or use Overtaxed IL to automate the entire process.
