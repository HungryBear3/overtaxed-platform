If you own property in Bloom Township, 2026 is your year. The Cook County Assessor is reassessing all properties in the South District — including Bloom Township — which means your assessed value is being recalculated, and your appeal window is now open.

## What is Bloom Township?

Bloom Township covers Chicago Heights, Steger, Lynwood, Glenwood, Olympia Fields, and surrounding communities in the south suburbs of Cook County.

## Why You Should Appeal in 2026

Reassessment years are the most important years to appeal. Here's why: the Assessor is actively recalculating values, which means your assessment could jump significantly — especially with recent market changes. An appeal lets you push back with real data.

In 2025, Cook County homeowners who appealed their assessments saved an average of $1,200+ per year. That's money back in your pocket.

## How to Check if You're Overassessed

Your assessed value should be roughly 10% of your home's fair market value (before the state equalization factor). If it's higher than that relative to comparable homes that have sold recently, you likely have grounds for an appeal.

## The Appeal Process for Bloom Township

1. **Get your reassessment notice** — mailed by the Cook County Assessor's Office when your township opens
2. **Check your appeal deadline** — you have 30 days from the date on your notice to file with the Assessor
3. **Gather comparable sales** — recent sales of similar properties in your area that sold for less than your assessed value implies
4. **File your appeal** — submit online at the Cook County Assessor's website, or let Overtaxed IL handle it for you
5. **Wait for the decision** — typically 60-90 days
6. **Appeal to Board of Review if needed** — a second chance if the Assessor doesn't reduce enough

## Don't Miss the Deadline

Once the Bloom Township appeal window closes, you can't appeal your 2026 assessment at the Assessor's level. You'd have to wait for the Board of Review window or until the next reassessment cycle.
