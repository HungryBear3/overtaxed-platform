If you own property in Cook County, Illinois, you have a limited window each year to appeal your assessment — and missing it means paying whatever the Assessor says, no questions asked.

## Why Deadlines Matter So Much

The Cook County Assessor reassesses properties on a rotating schedule by township. Once your township's appeal window closes, it's closed. No exceptions. No extensions.

The good news: if you're in a South District township, 2026 is your reassessment year — meaning your assessed value is being recalculated right now, and your appeal window is coming up.

## 2026 South District Townships in Reassessment

These townships are currently in the 2026 reassessment cycle:

- **Bloom**
- **Bremen**
- **Calumet**
- **Rich**
- **Thornton**
- **Worth**

Additional South District townships will open throughout spring and summer 2026.

## How the Appeal Process Works

There are two levels of appeal in Cook County:

**1. Cook County Assessor's Office**
This is your first opportunity. Once your reassessment notice arrives, you typically have 30 days to file. This is the most important window — appeals at this level tend to result in the largest reductions.

**2. Cook County Board of Review**
If you're not satisfied with the Assessor's decision (or missed the Assessor window), you can appeal to the Board of Review. They have separate deadlines, usually a few months after the Assessor's window closes.

## What You Need to Appeal

A successful appeal needs to show your assessed value is higher than comparable properties. You'll need:

- Your property's PIN (Property Index Number)
- Recent sales data for similar nearby properties
- Evidence of any property defects or unique circumstances
