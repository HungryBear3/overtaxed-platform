When you appeal your Cook County property tax assessment, the strongest argument you can make isn't an opinion about what your home is worth — it's hard data from recent sales of comparable properties. That data is called **Rule 15 evidence**, and it's the foundation of most successful appeals.

## What Is Rule 15?

Rule 15 refers to a rule of the Cook County Assessor's Office that governs how comparable sales can be used as evidence in an appeal. Specifically, it allows homeowners to submit data from recent arm's-length sales of similar properties to argue their home is over-assessed.

The logic is straightforward: if three similar homes in your neighborhood sold in the last 12 months for $350,000, but your assessment implies a market value of $420,000, you have a strong argument that the Assessor got it wrong.

## Why Comparable Sales Are the Gold Standard

Cook County assessments are supposed to reflect 10% of your property's fair market value. The best evidence of market value is what buyers actually paid for similar homes in the same area.

Other arguments exist — unequal assessment (comparing your assessed value to neighbors'), or an error in the property's characteristics (wrong square footage, wrong lot size) — but comparable sales are widely considered the most persuasive evidence at both the Assessor level and the Board of Review.

## What Makes a Good Comparable?

Not every recent sale qualifies. Good Rule 15 comparables should be:

- **Recent** — typically within 12 months of your assessment date, ideally within 6
- **Arm's-length** — normal market sales between unrelated buyers and sellers (not foreclosures, short sales, or family transfers)
- **Similar** — same general property type (single-family, condo), similar size, similar age, same township
- **Proximate** — within your neighborhood or close by

The Assessor's Office and Board of Review will scrutinize your comparables. Weaker ones (too old, too different in size) can hurt your case.

## How Many Comparables Do You Need?

Three to five solid comparables is typically enough to make a compelling case. More isn't always better — five strong comparables beat ten mediocre ones.

## How to Find Comparable Sales

**Option 1: Cook County Assessor's website.** The CCAO site has a property search tool that shows recent sales in any area. Search by neighborhood or township.

**Option 2: Zillow or Redfin.** Filter for recently sold homes similar to yours. Note: these pull from MLS data and may have slight delays.

**Option 3: Your county recorder.** All real estate transactions are recorded publicly. This is the authoritative source but requires more digging.

## What to Include in Your Rule 15 Submission

When you file, your comparables package should include:

1. The address and PIN of each comparable property
2. The sale price and sale date
3. Key property characteristics (square footage, bedrooms, lot size)
4. A brief explanation of why each property is comparable to yours

The Assessor's Office has standardized forms for this. At the Board of Review level, there's a specific comparable grid format they require.
