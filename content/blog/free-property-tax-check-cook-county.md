Most Cook County homeowners have no idea whether their property tax assessment is accurate. They get the bill, pay it, and move on. But a significant portion — especially in reassessment years — are paying more than they should.

Here's how to check in 60 seconds, for free.

## What Is a Property Tax Assessment?

Your property taxes are based on your **assessed value**, which the Cook County Assessor sets (typically at around 10% of your estimated market value). That assessed value is then multiplied by a state equalization factor and your local tax rate to produce your bill.

If the Assessor overestimates your property's value, your taxes go up — even if you haven't done anything to increase the value of your home.

## The Fastest Way to Check: Comparable Sales

The most reliable way to know if you're overpaid is to compare your assessed value to properties that have actually sold nearby. If similar homes sold for less than your assessment implies, you likely have grounds for an appeal.
