The Cook County Assessor accepts appeals based primarily on **comparable sales** — recent sales of similar nearby properties. If homes like yours sold for less than your assessed value implies, you have a strong case.

Here's how to find and use comparable sales effectively.

## What Makes a Good Comparable?

The ideal comparable property is:

- **Located nearby** — same neighborhood or subdivision if possible
- **Similar size** — within 10-20% of your square footage
- **Similar age and condition** — built within 10 years of yours, similar updates
- **Recently sold** — within the last 12 months is ideal; 24 months is acceptable
- **Similar use** — single-family compared to single-family, condo to condo

The more similar the comp, the stronger your argument.

## Where to Find Comparable Sales

**Cook County Assessor's website** — The Assessor publishes sales data publicly. You can search by PIN or address and see nearby sales.

**Redfin, Zillow, Realtor.com** — These aggregate MLS data and show recent sales with detailed information.

**Illinois Tax Assessor data** — Available through Overtaxed IL's free check tool, which pulls directly from county records.

**Your appeal packet** — Once you start an appeal, you typically need to submit 3-6 comparable sales as evidence.

## How Comps Connect to Your Assessed Value

In Cook County, the assessed value should be approximately 10% of market value. If comparable homes sold for $300,000, the correct assessed value would be around $30,000.

If your assessed value is $38,000 but comps suggest it should be $30,000, that's an $8,000 over-assessment — translating to roughly $1,000-1,500 in excess taxes per year depending on your tax rate.
