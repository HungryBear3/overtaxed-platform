The question every homeowner asks before starting a property tax appeal: is it actually worth it?

Short answer: almost always yes. Here's what the numbers look like.

## Average Savings for Cook County Homeowners

Based on Cook County Board of Review data, homeowners who appeal their assessments see reductions on a majority of cases filed. Average annual savings range from:

- **Single-family homes:** $800 – $2,500/year
- **Condos:** $400 – $1,200/year
- **Multi-unit properties:** $1,500 – $5,000+/year

These are annual savings — meaning every year until the next reassessment cycle (every 3 years in Cook County), you pay less.

## The Math on a Successful Appeal

Let's say you own a home assessed at $85,000 (triennial assessed value). The Assessor is claiming your home is worth $850,000.

If comparable homes in your area sold for $700,000, your assessed value should be closer to $70,000.

That $15,000 reduction in assessed value translates to roughly **$1,500–$2,000 less per year** on your tax bill, depending on your local tax rate.

## What Increases Your Chances of Saving More

**Strong comparable sales data.** The more recent and similar the sales, the stronger your case. Homes that sold in the same neighborhood, similar size, similar age, similar condition — within the last 12 months — carry the most weight.

**Reassessment years.** If your township is in a reassessment year (like South District townships in 2026), values are being recalculated fresh. This creates the biggest opportunity for corrections.

**Acting quickly.** Appeals filed early in the window tend to get more attention than last-minute filings. Don't wait until the deadline.
