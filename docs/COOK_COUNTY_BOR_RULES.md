# Cook County Board of Review Official Rules

Reference for OverTaxed IL. **Last updated:** June 24, 2025 (per Board of Review).

**Important:** Board of Review (BOR) Rule 15 governs *document submission requirements*, not comparable-property counts. The "3 sales + 5 equity comps" guidance comes from **Cook County Assessor** requirements, not BOR Rule 15.

---

## Rules Regarding Filing an Assessed Valuation Appeal

### Rule 1 — Who May Practice
Only licensed attorneys and individual taxpayers representing themselves (pro se) may practice before the Board. Individual taxpayers may represent properties titled in their name pro se or retain an attorney. Entities (corporations, LLCs, condominium associations, etc.) must be represented by an attorney.

**OverTaxed note:** DIY and full-automation users are typically pro se individual homeowners. Entity-owned properties require an attorney.

### Rule 2 — Attorney Authorization
Attorneys must file the Board's Attorney Authorization Form. Only the Board's original form may be submitted; it may not be altered. Must be submitted before finalization of the complaint or at submission. Late authorizations are not accepted.

### Rule 3 — Violations
Failure to follow any rule may be grounds for denial. Repeated/egregious violations may result in attorney code suspension or portal access revocation.

### Rule 4 — Form Completion
Each form must contain all applicable information and be completed in its entirety.

### Rule 5 — Official Complaint Form
Complaints must be filed on the official complaint form prescribed by the Board. All sections must be completed. **Facsimile filing is not permitted.**

**OverTaxed note:** We prepare evidence packets; users file the official form at the Assessor or BOR portal.

### Rule 6 — Duplicate Filing
The official complaint form must be filed in duplicate, unless filed online.

### Rule 7 — Township Deadlines
A complaint must be filed on or before the official closing date for the township. No complaint accepted after the township's filing period has closed.

**OverTaxed note:** We track township deadlines and remind users. Critical for both Assessor and BOR appeals.

### Rule 8 — Where to File
All complaints may be filed online via the Board's website. Otherwise, file at Board of Review, County Building, Room 601, 118 N. Clark Street, Chicago, IL 60602. Mail postmark date counts as filing date.

### Rule 9 — One Complaint Per Property
A separate complaint form must be filed for each property. If a property has two or more PINs, all related parcel numbers may be listed on one form.

### Rule 10 — Controlling Filings
- Attorney filing is controlling; subsequent complaints on same PIN are void.
- Pro se subsequent filings on the same PIN are controlling.

### Rules 11–13 — Hearings
Hearings are held at Board-set times. No documents need be presented at the hearing (evidence deadline has passed). Late evidence generally not accepted. Failure to appear waives oral argument.

## Rules Regarding Evidence

### Rule 14 — Document Deadline
All parties must file documents by the time period set by the Board following township close.

**Class 2 residential:** Brief, Historical Summary Form, and Assessor copies are **not required**.

### Rule 15 — Documents to File
- If appeal was filed before the Assessor for the same tax year: copies of all documents submitted to the Assessor. (**Not applicable for class 2 residential.**)
- Such other documents required by these rules, including a brief/letter and pertinent documents. (**Brief/letter not applicable for class 2 residential.**)
- For paper: documents bound at top with removable fastener.

**OverTaxed note:** BOR Rule 15 is about *what documents to submit*, not comp counts. Comparable-sales guidance (e.g., at least 3 sales) comes from Assessor requirements.

### Rule 16 — Signature
Complaints must be signed by the taxpayer or attorney. Signature certifies facts are true and correct.

### Rule 17 — Affidavits
Affidavits must be signed by a person with knowledge of the facts.

### Rule 18 — Purchase Price Disclosure
If the property was purchased within three years of the lien date, disclose purchase price, date of purchase, and file relevant sales documents. Disclose seller/buyer identity, any relationship between them, and whether arms-length.

**OverTaxed note:** Our filing authorization form captures purchased/refinanced and purchase price for this requirement.

### Rules 19–25 — Special Cases
- Rule 19: Appraisals must include front photo and PIN of subject and comps.
- Rule 20: Leased properties — Schedule E, income/expense statements.
- Rule 21: Vacancy relief — Vacancy affidavit, photos, listing/ads.
- Rule 22: Demolition — permit, payment, before/after photos.
- Rule 23: Excess vacant land — plat of survey, photos.
- Rule 24: Land trusts — schedule of beneficial owners.
- Rule 25: Intervenor complaints — same standards apply.

### Rule 26 — Re-Review
Dissatisfied taxpayers may request Re-Review (motion to reconsider). Must be filed within 3 days of Board decision letter. Must state specific grounds (new evidence, error in law, math error, etc.). Not for new legal theories.

### Rule 27 — Administrative No Change
Preserves right to appeal at Circuit Court or PTAB. Not reviewed on merits. Not entitled to Re-Review.
