# Illinois Expungement / Sealing — Petition Checklist

Complete these steps in order. Most petitions take 4–6 months from filing to final order.

---

## PHASE 1 — GATHER YOUR RECORDS (Before You File)

- [ ] **Request your official Illinois criminal history**
  - Illinois State Police: isp.illinois.gov → "Criminal History" → Background Check
  - Fee: ~$30 (online) or by mail
  - This is the record you're trying to clear — you need to know exactly what's on it

- [ ] **Get your Circuit Court case records**
  - Go to the Circuit Court Clerk in the county where you were convicted/arrested
  - Request copies of: arrest record, disposition (final outcome), sentence details
  - Fee varies by county ($5–$30)

- [ ] **Confirm case numbers and dates**
  - You'll need exact case numbers for the petition
  - Note: date of arrest, date of conviction/dismissal, offense, sentence, and end-of-sentence date

- [ ] **Identify all jurisdictions**
  - If you have records in multiple counties, you'll need to file separately in each one

---

## PHASE 2 — CONFIRM ELIGIBILITY

- [ ] Review Doc 1 (Eligibility Guide) against your specific offense and dates
- [ ] Confirm waiting period has passed
- [ ] Confirm no new convictions during waiting period
- [ ] If unsure: contact illinoislegalaid.org (free legal help online) or a local legal aid office

---

## PHASE 3 — COMPLETE THE PETITION

- [ ] Download the official Illinois petition form:
  - Illinois Courts: illinoiscourts.gov → Forms → Expungement
  - Form number: **ECLG-EX0001.1** (Petition to Expunge) or **ECLG-SL0001.1** (Petition to Seal)

- [ ] Fill in (use the template in Doc 3):
  - Your full legal name, date of birth, current address
  - All case numbers, counties, dates
  - Exact charges and dispositions
  - Basis for eligibility (arrest without conviction, supervision completed, etc.)

- [ ] Make **4 copies** of the completed petition:
  1. Original → Circuit Court Clerk (to file)
  2. State's Attorney's office (required service)
  3. Chief of the arresting agency (e.g., Chicago Police Dept., Sheriff)
  4. Your records copy

---

## PHASE 4 — FILE THE PETITION

- [ ] File original petition at the Circuit Court Clerk of the county of conviction
- [ ] Pay filing fee (approximately $0–$120 — varies by county; some counties waive for financial hardship)
- [ ] Court will stamp your petition and assign a case number
- [ ] Ask the clerk for the scheduled hearing date (typically 30–90 days out)

---

## PHASE 5 — SERVE ALL REQUIRED PARTIES

Within the time specified by the court (usually within 10–30 days of filing):

- [ ] Serve the **State's Attorney's Office** of the county of conviction
  - Deliver or mail a copy of your petition
  - Keep proof of service (certified mail receipt or process server affidavit)

- [ ] Serve the **Chief of the arresting law enforcement agency**
  - If arrested by Chicago Police: serve Chicago PD Records Division
  - If arrested by County Sheriff: serve that county's Sheriff
  - If arrested by ISP: serve Illinois State Police

- [ ] File **Proof of Service** with the court after serving all parties

---

## PHASE 6 — THE HEARING

- [ ] Attend your scheduled court hearing (typically very brief — 5–15 minutes)
- [ ] Bring: petition copy, proof of service, any supporting documents
- [ ] The State's Attorney may object (rare for clear-cut cases) or not appear
- [ ] Judge grants the petition → issues an **Order of Expungement or Sealing**

---

## PHASE 7 — AFTER THE ORDER

- [ ] Obtain **certified copies** of the Order from the clerk (get at least 3)
- [ ] Send certified copies to:
  - Illinois State Police Bureau of Identification (mandatory — they update the central record)
  - Any local agency that has your records
- [ ] Wait 60–90 days for records to be updated
- [ ] Re-run a background check on yourself to confirm the record is cleared

## FREE HELP RESOURCES

- **Illinois Legal Aid Online:** illinoislegalaid.org — free forms, eligibility screener, legal help
- **Cabrini Green Legal Aid:** cgla.net — free expungement clinics (Chicago)
- **Prairie State Legal Services:** pslegal.org — covers most of Illinois outside Cook County
- **Metropolitan Tenants Organization:** free expungement help for renters
- **Expungement courts:** many counties hold periodic free expungement clinics — ask your county clerk
