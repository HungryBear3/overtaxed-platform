# Illinois Expungement & Sealing — Am I Eligible?

**An expunged record is destroyed. A sealed record is hidden from public view but kept by the state.** Either can open doors that a criminal record has closed.

---

## QUICK ELIGIBILITY CHECK

### EXPUNGEMENT (Record Destroyed)

You may be eligible to expunge if:

**Arrests without conviction:**
- [ ] You were arrested but NOT convicted (charges dropped, dismissed, acquitted)
- [ ] You completed a supervision sentence and the case was dismissed
- [ ] You completed court supervision for a minor offense and time has passed
- [ ] Waiting period: typically 5 years after end of sentence, or per specific offense rules

**Juvenile records:**
- Most juvenile records can be expunged after age 18 or 21 (varies by offense)

**Cannabis convictions (automatic):**
- Illinois law provides for automatic expungement of many cannabis-related offenses
- Check status at illinoislegalaid.org or through your county clerk

**Specific adult convictions:**
Some Class 4 felonies and lower misdemeanors may be eligible depending on the offense and waiting period. See Section 2 below.

---

### SEALING (Record Hidden From Public)

You may be eligible to seal if:

- [ ] You have a qualifying conviction (most misdemeanors and some felonies)
- [ ] Required waiting period has passed (typically 3 years after end of sentence)
- [ ] You have NOT had subsequent convictions during the waiting period

**Most common sealable offenses:**
- Class A, B, C misdemeanors (theft under $500, disorderly conduct, minor drug possession, etc.)
- Class 4 felonies (many qualify — see ineligible list below)
- Class 3 felonies (limited eligibility)

---

## INELIGIBLE OFFENSES (Cannot Be Expunged or Sealed)

The following convictions generally CANNOT be expunged or sealed in Illinois:

- ❌ DUI (any)
- ❌ Domestic violence offenses
- ❌ Sex offenses requiring registration (SORA)
- ❌ Most violent felonies (murder, aggravated battery, armed robbery)
- ❌ Animal cruelty
- ❌ Offenses against children
- ❌ Reckless driving (if reduced from DUI)
- ❌ Dog fighting
- ❌ Most Class 1 and Class 2 felonies

---

## WAITING PERIODS

| Situation | Wait Time |
|-----------|-----------|
| Arrest with no conviction | Eligible immediately after case closes |
| Court supervision (completed + dismissed) | 5 years after end of supervision |
| Qualified misdemeanor conviction (sealing) | 3 years after sentence complete |
| Class 4 felony (sealing, if eligible) | 3 years after sentence complete |
| Juvenile record | Age 18 or 21 depending on offense |

"Sentence complete" = last day of probation, parole, or imprisonment, whichever is later.

---

## ILLINOIS CLEAN SLATE ACT (Automatic Sealing)

As of 2023, Illinois is implementing automatic sealing for many qualifying offenses without requiring the individual to petition. Check:
- Illinois Prisoner Review Board: illinois.gov/agencies/prisoner-review-board
- ILAO (Illinois Legal Aid Online): illinoislegalaid.org

Even if automatic sealing applies, filing a petition ensures all records in all jurisdictions are addressed.

---

## WHERE SEALED/EXPUNGED RECORDS CAN STILL APPEAR

Even after sealing or expungement:
- Law enforcement can still access sealed records
- Some state licensing agencies may access them (nursing, teaching, social work)
- Federal background checks (FBI) may still show records depending on circumstances
- If you later commit another crime, sealed records may be considered
