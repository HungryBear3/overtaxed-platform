# Illinois Notice of Landlord Entry

**Use this notice to inform a tenant of your intent to enter the rental unit.** Illinois law requires reasonable notice before entry except in emergencies. Most leases specify 24 hours; some municipalities require more.

## ILLINOIS ENTRY RULES

**General Rule:** Illinois does not have a statewide statute specifying exact notice hours for residential landlords (unlike some states). However:

- **Standard practice and most leases:** 24 hours advance notice
- **Chicago RLTO (Chicago Municipal Code 5-12-050):** 2 days (48 hours) notice required unless tenant consents or emergency exists
- **Emergency entry:** No notice required when there is immediate danger to life, health, or property (e.g., fire, flooding, gas leak)

**Always check:**
1. Your lease agreement for any specified notice period
2. Your city/municipality for any local ordinance

---

## EMERGENCY ENTRY (No Notice Required)

You may enter without notice only in genuine emergencies:
- Fire or risk of fire
- Flooding or water leak causing structural damage
- Gas leak
- Medical emergency involving the tenant
- Other imminent threat to life or property

**Document emergency entries** immediately after the fact — date, time, reason, and what was done.

---

## TENANT REFUSAL OF ENTRY

If a tenant unreasonably refuses entry after proper notice:
- Document the refusal in writing (text, email, or letter)
- This may constitute a lease violation
- In Chicago: tenant refusal for a showing is addressed under the RLTO
