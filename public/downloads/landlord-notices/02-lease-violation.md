# Illinois 10-Day Notice to Cure Lease Violation or Quit

**Use this notice when a tenant has violated a lease term (other than non-payment of rent).** Under Illinois law (735 ILCS 5/9-210), landlords must give tenants written notice and an opportunity to correct the violation before filing for eviction.

## HOW TO SERVE THIS NOTICE

Use one of these legally valid methods:

1. **Personal delivery** to the tenant directly
2. **Leave with a person of suitable age** at the rental unit
3. **Post on main door AND mail** via first-class mail (if no one answers)

Document the method and date of service on your copy.

---

## AFTER THE 10 DAYS

- **If cured:** The violation is resolved. Document that it was corrected with photos and a written note in your records.
- **If not cured:** You may proceed to file an Eviction Complaint with the Circuit Court in the county where the property is located.

---

## IMPORTANT: COOK COUNTY RLTO

If the property is in Chicago or another Cook County municipality that has adopted the Residential Landlord and Tenant Ordinance (RLTO):
- You may be required to give **additional notice** depending on the violation type
- Retaliatory eviction protections are stricter
- Consult the Chicago RLTO (Chicago Municipal Code 5-12) for city properties
