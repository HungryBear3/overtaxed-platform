# Illinois Eviction Prep Guide — What Comes After the Notice

**Your notices are served. The tenant didn't comply. Here's what happens next.**

---

## OVERVIEW: THE ILLINOIS EVICTION PROCESS

Illinois eviction (legally called "Forcible Entry and Detainer") proceeds in stages:

```
Serve Required Notice
        ↓
Notice Period Expires (5 or 10 days)
        ↓
File Eviction Complaint at Circuit Court
        ↓
Tenant is Served Summons (by sheriff or process server)
        ↓
Court Hearing (typically 14–21 days after filing)
        ↓
Judgment for Possession (if you win)
        ↓
Order of Possession Issued
        ↓
Sheriff Enforces Removal (if tenant doesn't leave voluntarily)
```

---

## STEP 1 — CONFIRM YOUR NOTICE IS VALID

Before filing, verify:
- [ ] You used the correct notice type (5-day for non-payment, 10-day for violations, 30-day for no-cause)
- [ ] Notice was properly served (personal delivery, leave at door + mail, or posting)
- [ ] Notice period has fully expired (count from the day AFTER service)
- [ ] You did not accept rent or make agreements after the notice that could void it
- [ ] Your lease is valid and signed

---

## STEP 2 — GATHER YOUR DOCUMENTS

Bring to court:
- [ ] Original signed lease agreement
- [ ] Copy of the notice(s) served + proof of service
- [ ] Rent ledger showing payment history and balance due
- [ ] Bank statements or receipts showing what was paid
- [ ] Photos of any property damage (if applicable)
- [ ] Any written communications with the tenant
- [ ] Your government-issued ID

---

## STEP 3 — FILE THE EVICTION COMPLAINT

**Where to file:**
Circuit Court of the county where the property is located.

**What to file:**
- Eviction Complaint form (available at the courthouse or county website)
- Copy of the notice you served
- Copy of the lease

**Filing fees:** Approximately $150–$300 depending on county

**After filing:**
The court will issue a Summons and assign a hearing date, typically 14–21 days out.

## STEP 4 — SERVING THE TENANT

The court will arrange service of the summons via the sheriff's office (standard) or you can hire a private process server. Fee: ~$25–$75.

The tenant must be properly served before the hearing. If service fails, the hearing may be postponed.

---

## STEP 5 — THE COURT HEARING

Arrive early. Bring your organized document binder.

**Likely outcomes:**
- **Default judgment** (tenant doesn't appear) → you win automatically; request order of possession
- **Agreed order** → tenant agrees to leave by a date; stipulated judgment entered
- **Contested hearing** → both sides present; judge rules

**If you win:**
The court issues a **Judgment for Possession**. The tenant has a short window to vacate (typically 7–14 days).

---

## STEP 6 — ORDER OF POSSESSION & SHERIFF ENFORCEMENT

If the tenant doesn't leave after the judgment:
1. Return to court and request an **Order of Possession**
2. Take the order to the Sheriff's office and pay the enforcement fee (~$100–$200)
3. Sheriff will schedule a lockout (typically within 7–21 days)

**You CANNOT:**
- Change locks without a court order
- Remove tenant's belongings without a court order
- Shut off utilities to force a tenant out
- These are illegal "self-help evictions" — they expose you to significant liability

---

## RECOVERING UNPAID RENT

An eviction judgment gives you possession. To collect unpaid rent:
- File a separate **Small Claims complaint** for the money owed (use the Small Claims Kit)
- Or request a combined judgment for possession AND rent at the eviction hearing

Judgments are good for 7 years and can be renewed. Use wage garnishment, bank levy, or property lien to collect.

---

## COOK COUNTY / CHICAGO RLTO ADDITIONAL RULES

Chicago landlords have extra obligations:
- **Security deposit interest:** Must pay annual interest on deposits held >6 months
- **Move-in/move-out checklists:** Required; failure affects deposit deductions
- **Notice requirements:** Some situations require longer notice under the RLTO
- **Just cause eviction (City of Chicago):** As of 2024, Chicago requires just cause for non-renewal in certain situations — verify current ordinance
