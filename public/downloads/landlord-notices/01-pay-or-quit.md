# Illinois 5-Day Notice to Pay Rent or Quit

**Use this notice when a tenant has not paid rent.** Under Illinois law (735 ILCS 5/9-209), a landlord must serve a written 5-day notice before filing for eviction for non-payment of rent.

## HOW TO SERVE THIS NOTICE (Illinois Law Requirements)

Service must be made by ONE of the following methods:

**Option 1 — Personal Delivery**
Hand the notice directly to the tenant. This is the most reliable method.

**Option 2 — Leave at Premises**
Leave the notice with a person of suitable age and discretion at the rental unit.

**Option 3 — Post and Mail**
If no one is home: post the notice on the main entry door AND mail a copy to the tenant via first-class mail.

**Document your service:**
- Write the date, time, and method of service on your copy
- If mailing, keep the mailing receipt
- Keep a copy for your records — you will need it in court

---

## IMPORTANT NOTES

- The 5-day period begins the **day after service** (not the day of service)
- Sundays and holidays DO count toward the 5 days in Illinois
- If the tenant pays in full during the 5 days, the notice is void
- If the tenant does NOT pay, you may file an **Eviction Complaint** with the Circuit Court
- **Do not accept partial payment** after the 5 days expire without a written agreement — it can restart the clock
- Cook County has additional requirements — see the Cook County Residential Tenant and Landlord Ordinance (RLTO) if applicable
