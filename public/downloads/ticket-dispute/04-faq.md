# FAQ — Illinois Traffic Ticket Disputes

---

**Q: What are my chances of winning?**

Better than most people think. Camera ticket dismissal rates in Chicago have historically run 30–50% for contested tickets — and that's including people who show up with no evidence. With a documented, organized dispute, your odds improve significantly. Moving violations are harder but winnable with the right evidence.

---

**Q: Will contesting a ticket hurt me?**

No. The worst outcome of contesting is that the original fine stands. You cannot be penalized for exercising your right to contest. Your insurance is not affected by an administrative adjudication outcome (it can be affected by a moving violation conviction in Circuit Court — different process).

---

**Q: Do I have to appear in person?**

For most Chicago parking and camera tickets: no. You can contest entirely by mail or online, and the decision is made on paper. You'll only appear if a hearing is scheduled (which you'll be notified of) or if you choose to attend.

---

**Q: What if I miss my court/hearing date?**

A default judgment will be entered against you. You can sometimes request a rehearing if you had a valid reason for missing — file immediately and explain the circumstance. Don't ignore it.

---

**Q: Can I contest a red light camera ticket if I wasn't driving?**

Yes. File an Affidavit of Non-Liability identifying who was actually driving. Chicago's ordinance allows the registered owner to transfer liability to the actual driver. The form is available on the Chicago Dept. of Finance website.

---

**Q: How long does the process take?**

- Online/mail contest decision: typically 30–90 days
- In-person hearing: same day decision usually
- Circuit Court appeal: 3–6 months

---

**Q: What if I can't afford to pay even if I lose?**

Don't ignore it — that makes it worse. Chicago and most Illinois municipalities offer:
- Payment plans (usually interest-free)
- Hardship programs (income-based reduction)
- Amnesty programs (offered periodically — watch for announcements)

Call the issuing department and ask. They'd rather collect something than nothing.

---

**Q: Will unpaid tickets affect my license?**

Yes. Illinois suspends driver's licenses for unpaid traffic debt and denies city sticker renewal in Chicago. If your license is already suspended for unpaid tickets, look into the Illinois Secretary of State's reinstatement process or consult a traffic attorney.

---

**Q: Are red light cameras even legal in Illinois?**

Yes — the Illinois Supreme Court has upheld them. However, individual citations can still be dismissed on procedural grounds (calibration, signage, yellow light timing). Challenging the camera system itself won't work; challenging your specific ticket on the merits can.

---

**Q: What's the difference between administrative adjudication and Circuit Court?**

- **Administrative adjudication** (city hearing): faster, cheaper, more informal — handles parking and camera tickets
- **Circuit Court**: handles moving violations issued by police officers, requires appearing before a judge
- A loss in administrative adjudication can be appealed to Circuit Court within 35 days
