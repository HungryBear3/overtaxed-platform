# Step-by-Step Illinois Property Tax Appeal Filing Instructions

---

## Step 1: Find Your Assessed Value and PIN

1. Locate your most recent property tax bill (mailed in spring)
2. Find your **Property Index Number (PIN)** — a 14-digit number
3. Look up your current assessed value at your county assessor's website
4. Calculate your implied market value:
   - **Cook County:** Assessed value ÷ 0.10 = implied market value
   - **All other Illinois counties:** Assessed value ÷ 0.3333 = implied market value

---

## Step 2: Know Your Deadline

**This is critical — missing the deadline means waiting an entire year.**

- Cook County: Each township has its own appeal window (typically 30 days after assessment notices are mailed). Check [cookcountyassessor.com](https://www.cookcountyassessor.com) for current open townships.
- Collar counties: Contact your county Board of Review directly. Most open in fall.
- See the **County Deadline Calendar** in your packet for specifics.

---

## Step 3: Gather Your Evidence

Use the **Evidence Checklist** in your packet. At minimum, prepare:
- 3–5 comparable sales
- A copy of your property record card (to check for errors)

---

## Step 4: File Your Appeal

### Cook County — Board of Review (Recommended First Step)

1. Go to [cookcountyboardofreview.com](https://www.cookcountyboardofreview.com)
2. Click "File an Appeal Online"
3. Create an account or log in
4. Enter your PIN
5. Fill out the appeal form — select your grounds (overvaluation, uniformity, or data error)
6. Upload your evidence (PDF format preferred, max file size varies)
7. Submit and save your confirmation number

**Alternative:** File by mail or in person at the Board of Review office (118 N. Clark Street, Chicago).

---

### Collar Counties (DuPage, Lake, Will, Kane, McHenry, etc.)

Each collar county has its own Board of Review. General process:

1. Contact your county's Board of Review (see calendar for contact info)
2. Obtain the appeal form (usually available on the county website)
3. Complete the form with your PIN, current AV, and proposed AV
4. Attach your evidence
5. File before the deadline — mail or in person depending on the county
6. Keep a copy of everything you submit

---

### State-Level Appeal: Property Tax Appeal Board (PTAB)

If your county Board of Review denies your appeal, you can escalate to PTAB.

- PTAB is the state-level appeals body
- You must file within 30 days of the Board of Review's decision
- File at: [ptab.illinois.gov](https://ptab.illinois.gov)
- PTAB hearings can take 1–3 years but are worth it for large reductions
- Consider hiring a tax attorney or consultant for PTAB appeals

---

## Step 5: After Filing

**What to expect:**
- **Confirmation:** You should receive a confirmation number or letter within 1–2 weeks
- **Hearing (sometimes):** Some boards schedule hearings; most are decided on paper
- **Decision:** Usually mailed 2–6 months after filing
- **If approved:** Your reduction will appear on the following year's tax bill (sometimes the current year)

---

## Common Mistakes That Get Appeals Denied

❌ **Missing the deadline** — No exceptions. Set a calendar reminder.

❌ **No evidence** — "I think it's too high" doesn't work. Bring data.

❌ **Wrong comps** — Using homes that are much larger, newer, or in a different neighborhood undermines your case.

❌ **Incomplete forms** — Make sure PIN, address, and AV figures are correct. Errors can invalidate your filing.

❌ **Not keeping copies** — Keep a copy of everything you submit.

❌ **Giving up after one denial** — County denial → PTAB is always an option.

---

## Tips for a Strong Appeal

✅ File early in the appeal window — boards get backlogged near deadlines

✅ Be professional and organized — label exhibits, use clear numbers

✅ Request a hearing if available — presenting in person can help

✅ If your reduction is large (>$50k in market value), consider a professional consultant who works on contingency (they take a % of your savings, no upfront cost)
