# Illinois Homestead Exemption Guide
## Stop Overpaying Property Taxes — You May Be Missing Free Money

## What Is the Homestead Exemption?

If you own and live in your home in Illinois, you are entitled to a **General Homestead Exemption** that automatically reduces your property's assessed value — lowering your tax bill every year.

**The catch:** In many Illinois counties, you have to apply. Thousands of homeowners miss it entirely and overpay by hundreds or thousands of dollars per year.

## Step 1: Check If You Already Have It

Before applying, verify whether the exemption is already on your account:

**Cook County:**
1. Go to [cookcountyassessor.com](https://www.cookcountyassessor.com)
2. Enter your address or PIN
3. Look for "General Homestead Exemption" in your exemptions list

**Other Illinois Counties:**
1. Go to your county assessor's website (search "[county name] assessor Illinois")
2. Look up your property by address or PIN
3. Check the exemptions or deductions listed

If you see it listed — you're already getting it. If not, keep reading.

---

## Step 2: Confirm You Qualify

You qualify for the General Homestead Exemption if:
- ✅ You **own** the property (includes land trusts, life estates, certain leases)
- ✅ You use it as your **primary residence** (you live there — not a rental or second home)
- ✅ You have occupied it as of **January 1** of the tax year

That's it. No income limit, no age limit.

---

## Step 3: Apply

### Cook County
1. Go to [cookcountyassessor.com](https://www.cookcountyassessor.com) → Exemptions → Homeowner Exemption
2. Apply online with your PIN and supporting documents
3. **Or** download the paper form and mail to: Cook County Assessor's Office, 118 N. Clark St., Room 320, Chicago, IL 60602
4. You'll need: proof of ownership (deed or tax bill) + proof of residency (driver's license or utility bill showing the property address)

**Deadline:** Applications are typically due in the spring — check the CCAO website for the current year's deadline.

### Other Illinois Counties
1. Contact your county assessor's office directly (usually by phone or their website)
2. Ask for the "General Homestead Exemption application"
3. Submit with proof of ownership and residency
4. Most counties accept applications year-round but apply them to the next assessment cycle

## Other Exemptions You May Be Missing

Once you're checking, look for these too — they stack on top of the general exemption:

### Senior Citizens Homestead Exemption
- **Who:** Age 65+ who own and occupy the property
- **Cook County savings:** additional $8,000 off assessed value (~$500–$1,000/yr more)
- **No income limit**

### Senior Citizens Assessment Freeze (SCAFR)
- **Who:** Age 65+, household income under $65,000
- **What it does:** Freezes your assessed value — it can never go up as long as you qualify
- **This is the big one for seniors** — massive savings over time as property values rise

### Persons with Disabilities Exemption
- **Who:** Owner-occupied, primary residence, proof of disability
- **Cook County:** $2,000 off assessed value

### Veterans with Disabilities Exemption
- **Who:** Veterans with service-connected disability rating from VA
- **Savings:** Ranges from partial reduction to 100% property tax exemption depending on disability rating
- **100% disabled veterans pay zero property taxes in Illinois**

## What If I Missed It for Prior Years?

In Cook County, you can apply for **back exemptions** for up to **3 prior tax years** if you qualified but didn't receive the exemption. This is called a Certificate of Error (CE) application.

A successful back-application can result in a refund check. It's worth pursuing.
