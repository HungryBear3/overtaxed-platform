# Illinois Divorce Prep — Document Checklist

**Gather these documents before you meet with an attorney or file anything.** Having everything organized upfront saves you hours of attorney time (and hundreds of dollars).

This checklist covers uncontested and contested divorce in Illinois.

---

## PERSONAL IDENTIFICATION

- [ ] Valid government-issued ID (driver's license, passport)
- [ ] Social Security card (yours and spouse's if available)
- [ ] Birth certificates (yours, spouse's, and any children)
- [ ] Marriage certificate (certified copy — get from the county clerk where you married)
- [ ] Prenuptial or postnuptial agreement (if any)

---

## FINANCIAL DOCUMENTS — INCOME

- [ ] Last 3 years of federal and state tax returns (both spouses)
- [ ] Last 6 months of pay stubs (both spouses)
- [ ] Most recent W-2s and 1099s
- [ ] Social Security benefit statements (if applicable)
- [ ] Proof of any other income: rental income, business distributions, alimony from prior marriage, disability payments

---

## FINANCIAL DOCUMENTS — ASSETS

**Bank Accounts:**
- [ ] Statements for all checking and savings accounts — last 12 months (all accounts, all banks)
- [ ] List of all accounts with account numbers and institution names

**Retirement Accounts:**
- [ ] Most recent statements for all 401(k), 403(b), IRA, pension accounts
- [ ] Note the date of marriage and what was contributed before vs. during marriage (marital vs. non-marital)

**Investment Accounts:**
- [ ] Brokerage account statements (last 12 months)
- [ ] Stock certificates or ESOP records

**Real Estate:**
- [ ] Deed to marital home and any other real property
- [ ] Most recent mortgage statement (balance, monthly payment, lender)
- [ ] Most recent property tax bill
- [ ] Home appraisal (if recent) or estimated value from Zillow/Redfin
- [ ] Any rental property leases and income records

**Vehicles:**
- [ ] Titles for all vehicles (cars, motorcycles, boats, RVs)
- [ ] Loan statements for any financed vehicles

**Business Interests:**
- [ ] Business ownership documents (articles of incorporation, LLC operating agreement, partnership agreement)
- [ ] Last 3 years of business tax returns
- [ ] Business bank account statements

**Other Assets:**
- [ ] Life insurance policies with cash value (surrender value statement)
- [ ] Appraisals for jewelry, art, or collectibles of significant value
- [ ] Cryptocurrency holdings (wallet statements or exchange records)
- [ ] Inheritance or gift documentation (received before or during marriage)

---

## FINANCIAL DOCUMENTS — DEBTS

- [ ] Most recent statements for all credit cards (both individual and joint)
- [ ] Mortgage statement(s)
- [ ] Home equity loan or HELOC statements
- [ ] Auto loan statements
- [ ] Student loan statements (federal and private)
- [ ] Personal loan documents
- [ ] Any judgments or liens against you or your spouse
- [ ] List of any money owed to family members (informal loans)

---

## CHILDREN (If Applicable)

- [ ] Children's birth certificates
- [ ] School records (current school, grades, special needs documentation)
- [ ] Medical records and health insurance information
- [ ] Documentation of daycare or extracurricular activity costs
- [ ] Any existing court orders for custody or support from prior relationships
- [ ] Documentation of each parent's involvement in childcare, school, activities

---

## INSURANCE

- [ ] Health insurance cards and policy information (including who covers children)
- [ ] Life insurance policies (beneficiary designations)
- [ ] Auto insurance policies
- [ ] Homeowner's or renter's insurance policy

---

## HOUSEHOLD & LIVING EXPENSES

- [ ] Last 6 months of household bills: utilities, internet, phone, groceries, subscriptions
- [ ] Monthly expense summary (what it costs to run your household)

---

## LEGAL DOCUMENTS

- [ ] Any prior court orders related to the marriage (custody, support, protective orders)
- [ ] Wills and trusts for both spouses
- [ ] Power of attorney documents
- [ ] Any pending lawsuits involving either spouse

---

## DIGITAL / SOCIAL

- [ ] Screenshots of any social media posts relevant to financial assets, income, or conduct
- [ ] Records of any suspicious financial transfers or dissipation of marital assets
- [ ] Email records related to finances, assets, or children

---

## ORGANIZATION TIP

Create a folder (physical or Google Drive) with subfolders:
- Personal ID
- Income
- Bank/Investment Accounts
- Real Estate
- Retirement
- Debts
- Children
- Insurance

Being organized before your first attorney meeting can save 1–2 hours of billable time.
