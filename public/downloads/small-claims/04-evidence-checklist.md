# Illinois Small Claims — Evidence Checklist

**What you bring to court determines whether you win.** Use this checklist to gather everything before your hearing.

---

## GENERAL EVIDENCE (All Cases)

- [ ] **Your demand letter** — the one you sent before filing, with proof of delivery (certified mail receipt, email sent confirmation, or witness)
- [ ] **Timeline of events** — a simple 1-page chronology: what happened, in what order, with dates
- [ ] **All written communications** — texts, emails, letters, DMs. Print them out and highlight the key parts.
- [ ] **Photos or videos** — of damaged property, defective work, the item in question. Timestamp matters.
- [ ] **Receipts and invoices** — proving what you paid, when, and for what
- [ ] **Bank/payment records** — Venmo, Zelle, CashApp, check images, credit card statements

---

## BY CLAIM TYPE

### 🏠 Security Deposit Not Returned

- [ ] Signed lease agreement
- [ ] Proof of deposit payment (check, Venmo, bank statement)
- [ ] Move-in checklist / condition report (if you have one)
- [ ] Move-out photos (dated) — condition you left the unit in
- [ ] Any written communication with landlord about the deposit
- [ ] Proof of your new address (to show landlord had it)
- [ ] Note: If no itemized list was provided within 30 days, landlord may owe double the deposit (765 ILCS 710/1)

### 🔨 Contractor / Home Repair Dispute

- [ ] Signed contract or written estimate
- [ ] Proof of payments made
- [ ] Photos of the work — both what was promised and what was delivered
- [ ] Any text/email communications about the job
- [ ] Quotes from other contractors showing cost to fix the bad work
- [ ] Check if contractor is licensed: verify at idfpr.illinois.gov (required for jobs over $1,000)

### 💸 Unpaid Loan / Money Owed

- [ ] Written loan agreement (if any)
- [ ] Texts or emails where the debt is acknowledged
- [ ] Payment records showing what was originally transferred
- [ ] Any partial repayments (to show the balance remaining)

### 📦 Defective Product / Goods

- [ ] The product itself (if possible — it's physical evidence)
- [ ] Receipt / proof of purchase
- [ ] Warranty documentation
- [ ] Photos of the defect
- [ ] Any communications with the seller about returns/refunds
- [ ] Expert opinion if needed (mechanic, inspector, etc.)

### 🚗 Auto Accident / Property Damage

- [ ] Police report
- [ ] Photos of damage (from the scene and after)
- [ ] Repair estimate or invoice
- [ ] Insurance correspondence
- [ ] Witness names and contact info
- [ ] Medical records if injury is part of the claim (note: personal injury claims over $10K go to regular civil court)

---

## HOW TO ORGANIZE YOUR EVIDENCE

**Label everything as exhibits:**
- Exhibit A — Demand letter + proof of delivery
- Exhibit B — Signed contract
- Exhibit C — Proof of payment
- Exhibit D — Photos (print them, don't just show on phone)
- Exhibit E — Communications (texts/emails, printed)
- ... and so on

**Make 3 sets:**
1. For the judge
2. For the defendant
3. Your copy to refer to while speaking

**Create a one-page summary:** Date, amount claimed, what happened in 3–5 sentences. Hand it to the judge first — it helps them follow along.

---

## WITNESS TIPS

If you have a witness who supports your case:
- Ask them to appear in person if possible (far more effective than a written statement)
- If they can't appear, a signed, notarized affidavit is the next best thing
- Brief them on what you need them to say — specific facts, not opinions

---

## WHAT NOT TO BRING

- Unrelated grievances — stick to the claim you filed
- Hearsay ("my friend said they saw...") without the actual witness
- Emotional arguments — judges want facts and documents
- Anything you don't have a copy of (keep everything)
