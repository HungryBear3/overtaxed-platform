# Illinois Small Claims — Pre-Filing Demand Letter Template

**Send this BEFORE you file.** Courts look favorably on plaintiffs who attempted to resolve the dispute first. It also sometimes gets you paid without going to court at all.

## HOW TO SEND THIS LETTER

**Send via at least one of:**
- Certified mail with return receipt (creates a paper trail)
- Email (keep a copy of the sent email)
- Hand delivery with a witness present

**Keep a copy for court** — attach it as Exhibit A to your complaint if you end up filing.

---

## TIPS FOR AN EFFECTIVE DEMAND LETTER

- **Be factual, not emotional.** State what happened and what you're owed. Skip the anger.
- **Set a firm deadline.** 10–14 days is standard. Too short looks aggressive; too long signals you're not serious.
- **Don't threaten things you won't do.** If you say you'll file, mean it.
- **Reference Illinois law when relevant:**
  - Security deposits: 765 ILCS 710/1 (Landlord must return within 30–45 days)
  - Home repair fraud: 815 ILCS 515 (contractors over $1,000 must be licensed)
  - Consumer fraud: 815 ILCS 505 (deceptive practices may entitle you to attorney fees)
