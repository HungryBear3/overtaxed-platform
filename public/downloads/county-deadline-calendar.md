## Cook County Township Appeal Windows (Typical)

Cook County is unique — each of the 38 townships has its own appeal window. The CCAO sends out reassessment notices, and you have 30 days from your notice date to appeal.

**General pattern:**
- **City of Chicago townships:** Typically reassessed every 3 years on a rotating schedule
- **North/Northwest suburbs:** Different rotation years
- **South/Southwest suburbs:** Different rotation years

**Always check:** [cookcountyassessor.com](https://www.cookcountyassessor.com) → "Check Assessment Status" to see if your township is currently open.

---

## State-Level: Property Tax Appeal Board (PTAB)

- **When:** Within 30 days of your county Board of Review decision
- **Where:** [ptab.illinois.gov](https://ptab.illinois.gov)
- **Process:** File Form PTAB-1 with supporting evidence
- **Timeline:** 1–3 years (PTAB has a significant backlog)
- **Worth it for:** Large properties, significant overvaluations, commercial property

---

## Key Dates to Remember

| Event | Typical Timing |
|-------|---------------|
| Assessment notices mailed | Spring/Summer (varies by county) |
| Appeal window opens | Within 30 days of notice |
| Appeal window closes | **Hard deadline — no extensions** |
| Board hearing (if applicable) | 1–4 months after filing |
| Decision mailed | 2–6 months after filing |
| Tax bill reflects reduction | Following year's bill |
